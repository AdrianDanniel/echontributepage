Nikola Tesla: The Visionary Inventor

Nikola Tesla (1856–1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, engineer, and futurist, best known for his contributions to the development of alternating current (AC) electricity, as well as his numerous innovations in radio, wireless technology, and robotics. Tesla’s inventions paved the way for the future of electrical power and communications, shaping modern science and technology.

Born in Smiljan, Croatia, Tesla showed an early aptitude for electrical engineering and went on to work for renowned figures like Thomas Edison before establishing his own ventures. Despite numerous challenges and financial hardships, Tesla's work continued to inspire scientists and engineers worldwide, and his vision for a world interconnected by wireless technology remains influential to this day.

One of Tesla's most famous experiments involved the Tesla Coil, a device capable of producing high-voltage electricity and wireless energy transmission. This groundbreaking invention laid the foundation for modern technologies like radio, television, and even Wi-Fi.
